Team Ireland CanSat - Spudnik

Exploring the Atmosphere with Potato-Powered Innovation

Project Overview

Spudnik is an innovative CanSat project developed by Team Ireland for the European Space Agency's CanSat competition. Our mission is to design, build, and launch a small satellite capable of collecting atmospheric data using potato-based technology.

Technical Specifications

  • Dimensions: 66mm diameter, 115mm height
  • Weight: 350 grams
  • Power Source: Custom potato-based battery
  • Sensors: Temperature, pressure, humidity, GPS
  • Communication: 433 MHz radio transmitter

Mission Objectives

  1. Collect atmospheric data during descent
  2. Demonstrate the viability of potato-based power sources
  3. Achieve a soft landing using a custom parachute system
  4. Transmit data in real-time to the ground station

Project Timeline

Our project spanned over six months, from initial concept to final launch. Key milestones included prototype development, sensor integration, and rigorous testing phases.
