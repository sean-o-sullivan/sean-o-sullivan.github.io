Research & Projects

  • VerifyMe: An AI-based authorship verification system using stylometric features and a Siamese neural network to confirm a writer’s authentic style—even against advanced AI mimicry. Currently designing the third iteration.
  • ForceField: A wearable device for hurling that tracks potentially concussive impacts in real time. Features a custom PCB, integrated sensors, and a dedicated app, developed during a youth accelerator.
  • Wind Turbine Research: Conducted seven tests—both in the University of Limerick wind tunnel and outdoors—on 16 micro-scale turbine configurations (10 VAWT, 6 HAWT). Additionally tested a 500w domestic turbine and an interest survey with 170 adults. Used 3D-printed rotors, Arduino-based data capture, and anemometer readings to evaluate blade geometry, rotor scaling, in the context of domestic-scale wind generation.
  • RFID Cat Feeder: Built and refined an RFID-controlled feeder through four major iterations, improving coil design, reliability, and user interaction. The latest version logs feeding data in real time and restricts access to a specific microchip.
  • ClimateActionHow.com: Co-created an interactive website offering practical sustainability tips and educational tools, aiming to inspire everyday climate action.
  • Spudnik (CanSat): Engineered key components—3D-printed enclosures, PCBs, parachute, and a rigid-fold origami solar array—that led to first place at the 2023 Irish National Finals.
  • Pace: Designed a magnetically attachable minute timer for swimmers’ goggles, now on its third prototype, enabling precise lap pacing and interval tracking.
  • Clock: Constructed a mechanical seven-segment display clock with exposed wiring and servo-driven digits, installed in the school’s main area to spark curiosity about timekeeping.
